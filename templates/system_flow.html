System Flow & Architecture

High-level data flow and decision points in the DSS.

Overview

This page explains how data moves through the Student Mental Health Decision Support System (DSS) and highlights the decision points used by administrators and counselors.

  1. Data collection: Students submit monthly surveys (survey_responses).
  2. Scoring: Responses are processed to compute a `total_score` per survey and stored in `survey_summary`.
  3. Risk classification: Total scores are mapped to risk levels (Low, Medium, High) by the DSS engine.
  4. Admin review: Admins view aggregated dashboards and assign counselors for High/Medium cases.
  5. Counselor assignment & intervention: Counselors manage cases, add notes, and schedule appointments.
  6. Analytics: Trends and insights are generated from `survey_summary` to inform capacity planning and interventions.

Decision Points

  • Survey completeness check: ensure one survey per month before scoring.
  • Risk thresholds: determine Low/Medium/High boundaries.
  • Assignment policy: only one active assignment per student; balance workload.
  • Appointment prioritization: High risk -> immediate scheduling recommendations.

Notes for Presentations

  • Highlight how the DSS turns survey data into actionable assignments.
  • Show analytics charts to demonstrate trends and justify interventions.
  • Discuss fairness and workload balancing for counselors.
